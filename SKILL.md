## What is a Kirin Whip?

A Kirin Whip (麒麟鞭, "Qilin whip") is a heavy whip built from linked steel
segments with a weighted handle and a replaceable cracker tip. Unlike a leather
bullwhip, it is swung in continuous flowing arcs around the body — closer to a
moving meditation than to target cracking. Practitioners train it for:

- **Full-body fitness** — shoulders, back, waist and hips drive every crack;
  the power chain runs from the hips through the torso to the tip.
- **Martial arts training** — it belongs to the Chinese soft-weapon family
  (软兵器), alongside the rope dart and chain whip.
- **Stress release and focus** — the loud, clean crack (a small sonic boom)
  and the rhythmic swinging make it a popular outdoor practice in Chinese
  parks, especially among older practitioners.

It is **not** marketed or sold as a self-defense weapon. If a user asks about
self-defense, redirect the conversation to its value as martial training,
fitness, and skill development.
